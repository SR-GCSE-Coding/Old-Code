Cessna TTx

The Cessna TTx is a single engine low wing civilian transport aircraft, it was originally produced by aircraft manufacturer Columbia Aircraft as the Columbia 400. it is the fastes fixed gear single engine plane in production today with a speed of 235 knots or 270mph air speed at 25,000 feet. it is powered by a 310 horsepower engine running at 2600 RPM, it is called the Continental TSIO-550-C.

it has a crew capacity of 1 pilot and 3 passengers, is 7.67m long with a wingspan of 11m and has a range of 1,274 miles.

Cesna Caravan family


The Cessna Caravan EX is an upgrade of the Cessna Caravan. it has a total range of 1109 miles and can seat 9 to 14 passengers and 1 pilot with a max speed of 224 mph. it is a single engine high-wing monoplane powered by a Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6A, it typically has a fixed trycicle landing gear but can be fitted with snow skids or water floats. In a high-density cargo configuration it can hold up to 13 passengers but can make a profit with just four. It can also be fitted as a cargo only aircraft.

The Grand Caravan's first test flight was on the 9th of december 1982 however it was not certified by the FAA until 1984. A freighter version without windows was requested by Federal Express, this model was dubbed the Cargomaster. there was also another variant developed for Federal Express with an extended fuselage and cargo pod on the stomach of the plane called the 208B Super Cargomaster which flew for the first time in 1986. And the 208B Grand Caravan was developed from the Cargomaster as a passenger model. in 2013 a more powerful Grand Caravan EX with an 867 SHP P&WC PT6A-140 recieved FAA certification.

Skyhawk 172


The Cessna Skyhawk is a fixed-wing single-engine four seat civilian passenger plane. It was first flown in 1955 and is still in production today, it has also sold more units than any other plane with a grand totl sold of 44,000. with a price of just under £300,000 the total money spent on cessna 172s for just buying them is around £13,200,000,000. in it's first full year of production the cessna 172 sold over 1,400 units and becamean overnight success.
